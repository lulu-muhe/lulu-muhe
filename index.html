About

About Professor Lulu Muhe

Professor Lulu Muhe is a globally recognized pediatrician and public health researcher with over four decades of experience leading transformative child health initiatives in Ethiopia and internationally. He has played a pivotal role in the development and implementation of the Integrated Management of Childhood Illness (IMCI) strategy, worked extensively with WHO, and currently teaches and supervises postgraduate students at Addis Ababa University. for more info.

Research

Research & Leadership

Professor Muhe has successfully led multi-country research projects on pneumonia, diarrhea, newborn health, HIV, malaria, and pediatric diabetes. His leadership has translated research into actionable policy and practice, supported by reputable funders such as WHO and the Gates Foundation.

Policy Impact

Policy & Global Health Impact

He has been instrumental in crafting WHO guidelines, training tools, and national policy documents. His work has influenced programs across Africa and Asia in areas such as IMCI, neonatal care, HIV, and child health in emergencies.

publications

With over 170 peer-reviewed articles and multiple book chapters, Professor Muhe’s work is widely cited. Access his publications via ORCID https://orcid.org/0000-0002-2776-9923.

Teaching

Teaching & Mentorship

He supervises PhD, Master’s, and pediatric residents, and lectures in child health at Addis Ababa and Geneva universities. His teaching emphasizes critical thinking and research application.
